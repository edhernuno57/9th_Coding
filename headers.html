Understanding HTML Headers

What are HTML Headers?

HTML headers are used to define headings in a webpage. There are six levels of headers, from <h1> to <h6>, with <h1> being the most important and largest, and <h6> being the least important and smallest.

Why Use Different Header Levels?

Using different header levels helps organize content hierarchically, making it easier for both users and search engines to understand the structure of the webpage. For example, <h1> is typically used for the main title of the page, while <h2> through <h6> are used for subheadings of decreasing importance.
